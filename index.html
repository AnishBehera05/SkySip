About SkySip

SkySip is an innovative atmospheric water harvester designed to provide a sustainable solution to global water scarcity. By utilizing Calcium Chloride, a highly effective desiccant, and renewable energy sources, SkySip captures and condenses atmospheric moisture into potable water. Our device is particularly effective in high-humidity regions, such as coastal areas, where traditional water sources may be scarce or unreliable.

Problem Statement

Over 2 billion people globally lack access to safe drinking water due to overexploitation, pollution, and urbanization. SkySip addresses this issue by utilizing untapped atmospheric moisture as a sustainable water source. This innovative solution is especially vital in high-humidity areas, such as coastal regions, where the potential for water harvesting from the air is maximized.

Technology

The SkySip device integrates innovative materials and efficient electrical components to ensure reliable water harvesting.

Calcium Chloride: A widely available desiccant that absorbs atmospheric moisture.
Copper Plate: Optimized for condensation, cooled by energy-efficient condensers.
Arduino Microcontroller: Automates system operations for precision and efficiency.
Rack and Pinion Mechanism: Operates the acrylic cover for optimized humidity exposure.

How It Works

The SkySip device operates in a well-defined schedule for optimized water harvesting. Below is the detailed timetable for its operations:

10:05 AM

Condensation starts. The copper plate is cooled, and water droplets form as the system begins harvesting water.

11:05 AM

Fresh air is allowed to enter the system. This helps replenish the desiccant and improve efficiency.

12:05 PM

Condensation starts again after a delay to ensure optimal condenser performance.

1:05 PM

Fresh air is introduced to the system to maintain balance and enhance the desiccant's effectiveness.

2:05 PM

Condensation resumes, maximizing water collection during peak hours.

3:05 PM

Fresh air flows into the system, ensuring the desiccant remains effective.

4:05 PM

Condensation restarts for the final session of the day, continuing until 5 PM.

5:05 PM

Fresh air enters and continues to flow through the system until the next day at 10 AM.
